Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, the Father of the Nation, led India's fight for independence through nonviolence and civil disobedience. His Salt March in 1930 protested the British salt tax. He championed equality, uniting religious groups in the Khilafat Movement. Gandhi's Swadeshi Movement promoted indigenous goods and boycotted British products. The Quit India Movement in 1942 intensified pressure on British rule. His principles of truth and nonviolence influenced global civil rights movements. He negotiated with British authorities, shaping India's future. Gandhi's legacy promotes love and compassion for social change. His efforts led to India's independence in 1947. Unfortunately, he was assassinated in 1948 by a person with extreme beliefs. His influence remains an inspiration for justice and peaceful resistance worldwide.

Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose, a key freedom fighter, left an indelible mark on India's independence struggle. His assertive stance set him apart within the Indian National Congress. Leading the Forward Bloc, he advocated for a more forceful approach. Bose's mysterious disappearance in 1945 added intrigue to his story. His contributions include leading the Indian National Army (INA), which fought alongside Japanese forces to liberate India. Through Azad Hind Radio, his stirring speeches rallied Indians against colonial rule. His audacious escape from British house arrest in 1941 underscored his determination. Seeking Axis support during WWII, Bose established connections with leaders like Hitler. In 1943, he formed a Provisional Government of Free India in Singapore. The INA trials and public support influenced India's road to independence. His call, "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom," continues to inspire. Subhas Chandra Bose's dedication and vision for a strong, free India endure as a source of inspiration.

Kittur Rani Chennamma

Kittur Rani Chennamma was born on 14th November 1778, and she was the first women independence activist of Bharat. From a very young age, Kittur was trained in archery, sword fighting, and horse riding due to her family tradition. Kittur Rani Chennamma was married at the age of 15 to Raja Mallasaraja, who was a member of the Desai Family. Kittur Rani Chennamma is very famous for her 1824 revolt against the East India Company which lost the battle against her. Bhagat Singh

Bhagat Singh, a valiant Indian freedom fighter, embodied courage and sacrifice in the struggle against British rule:In 1928, he protested the Simon Commission, symbolizing public dissent and nationalism against colonial exploitation. Bhagat Singh's non-lethal bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929 aimed to draw attention to the demand for complete independence. His arrest and subsequent trial became a platform to articulate his vision for a free India, marked by his iconic slogan "Inquilab Zindabad." Executed in 1931 for his role in the killing of a British police officer, Singh's martyrdom ignited national fervor and intensified the fight for freedom. Through his newspaper "The People" and writings, he advocated for a socialist society, emphasizing justice and equality. Bhagat Singh's fearless spirit and sacrifice continue to inspire generations in the pursuit of justice, unity, and freedom..
